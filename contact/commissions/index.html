Commissions

The response to my Beholder videos, in particular, has been enthusiastic and I have received a lot of inquiries regarding commissioned work pricing and terms.

I really do love to collaborate on ideas and am motivated in this pursuit at least as much by exploration and discovery as anything else. There are intrinsically many unknowns and surprises when channeling the swirling gigabytes of chaos through these algorithms and I get inspired every time the system generates something new.

The software rendering times and limitations are in a state of flux as I look to optimize results and costs on various cloud providers. As such, it is difficult for me to make strong guarantees when it comes to project turnaround times and output quality at this stage.

However, if you are flexible and interested in experimenting with me, here's how we can get you your own Beholder video.

What's the goal?

We need to agree on the vision and usage context.

Define source video.

You will need to have the rights to the video if you're going to publish the results of what we make. If you're doing a music video but don't have a video yet, shoot yourself lip-syncing, playing instruments, or acting out some scenes with your phone. Don't worry if your scenery is boring, since Beholder will change all of that. Movement is good and close-ups are good.

Define the style.

Famous visual artists with unique styles are good to have in mind. I've got a lot of mileage with "HR Giger" for example. Things like "sunrise", "happy aliens", "neon lights", "porcelain statues" or "trees and vines" are examples of themes too. This is where we get really creative.

Frame sampling.

I'll run an initial sample of frames through the model and present the results for review so we can iterate on the style.

Clip sampling.

After agreeing on the style from the stills, I'll generate a few short clips to test the actual video experience of the chosen style.

Full video generation.

This can take a day or more depending the model settings, the dimensions and duration of the video, and the frames per second. I'll provide a watermarked version for review.
